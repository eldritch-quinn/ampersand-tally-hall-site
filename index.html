Tally Hall

Good & Evil

"&" is the second track of the album "Good & Evil" as well as their sixth most popular song.

It is a summation of the album's theme of duality and unlike the other tracks from Good & Evil, "&" is broader and deals with opposites in all its forms.

About Tally Hall

Tally Hall is an American rock band formed in Ann Arbor, Michigan, in December 2002, and publicly active until the conclusion of their Good & Evil tour in 2011. The band is known for its upbeat melodies and whimsical lyrics. The members originally described their musical style as "wonky rock", later redefining their sound as "fabloo" (/fəˈbluː/ fə-BLOO[1]), in an effort to not let their music be defined by any particular genres after critics began defining the characteristics of "wonky rock".[2]

"Turn the Lights Off" speaks of dreams and nightmares; "You and Me" is self-explanatory; etc. Unlike the other tracks from Good & Evil, "&" is broader and deals with opposites in all its forms.
